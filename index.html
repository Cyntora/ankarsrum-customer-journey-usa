03Where the USA sits
Two market types, two different journeys

The USA is a new starter market.
It does not yet behave like a mature one.

New starter markets · You are here
USA, United Kingdom
  • Awareness is still being built. Paid Search and PMax carry significant load because the brand has not saturated yet.
  • Customers compare and verify. They look at reviews, alternatives, refurbished, sale options. Price is part of the decision.
  • Aesthetic choice matters. Six US colour variants each pulled 8k to 19k sessions. The mixer is a visual purchase.
  • Price-sensitive sub-segments thrive. Refurbished and "ankarsrum mixer sale costco" searches show a parallel audience.
  • USA is the larger new starter, the UK is the smaller. The US funnel works at scale. The UK is still mostly in awareness phase.
Mature markets · For contrast
Sweden, Germany
  • Brand awareness is established. Direct visits and brand-name searches dominate the traffic mix.
  • Customers arrive with intent. The mixer purchase is the goal of the visit, not an exploration.
  • Content engages but doesn't always close. Recipe pages drive brand value across many years of return visits.
  • Conversion happens deep in the funnel. Home page and category page do most of the closing.
  • Email and direct visits over-convert. The customer relationship is already established.

The recommendations in this deck assume USA's new starter behaviour. The mixer is being discovered, not just bought.

Both numbers are real. They measure different things. The honest read is that Meta drives awareness and upper-funnel attention but rarely closes the sale on the same visit. That is a strategy question, not a measurement bug.

09What to do about it
Four actions, ordered by impact

Concrete moves the data supports.

1

Build a dedicated refurbished funnel

Treat refurbished as its own product line, not a sale page. Separate landing page, dedicated paid search campaign on "refurbished" and "used" terms, a Costco-comparison page that owns the search "ankarsrum mixer sale costco" (859 clicks a year already going there). Email flow for price-sensitive subscribers.

Medium effort · Hidden upside
2

Lean into colour-led merchandising

Six color variants already produced 653 purchases and 74,727 sessions. Make this explicit. Seasonal color launches, color-quiz on the home page, lifestyle photography per color, email segmentation by colour preference. The mixer is a kitchen object as much as a tool.

Low effort · Reuses existing engine
3

Reframe Paid Social as upper-funnel

Stop measuring Paid Social by direct GA4 conversion. Use it for reach, awareness, video views and email signups. Measure brand search volume and direct traffic 2 to 4 weeks after a Meta campaign launches. Move the conversion expectation to PMax, Paid Search and Email.

Reframe · Better expectations
4

Protect what works, scale carefully

Direct, Organic Search, Paid Search, PMax and Email all convert above 1 percent. Together they produced 7,070 purchases on twelve months. Do not break the engine. Scale PMax budget and email volume incrementally and measure marginal return.

Maintain · Strategic
10Suggested next phase
Building on these findings

What we recommend tackling next.

01

Three US personas, drawn from the data

The Brand Buyer (direct and brand search), the Refurbished Hunter (price-led, searching used and sale), and the Color Picker (browsing product variants). The data exists, it needs structuring and shared language for marketing decisions.

02

Refurbished funnel pilot

Dedicated landing, separate paid campaign, separate email cohort. Measure incremental purchases attributable to the refurbished line over 90 days against the current baseline of 170 purchases per year.

03

Color-led campaign and CRO test

A/B test a color-first home page or category page. Add a "find your color" path. Measure click-through to product variant pages and the share of purchases that come from variant pages versus the generic stand-mixer page.

04

Brand-lift measurement for Paid Social

Set up a proper measurement framework. Track branded search volume and direct traffic before, during and after Meta campaign flights. Replace the GA4 last-non-direct view with a multi-source attribution model for awareness channels.

12Sources and caveats
Transparency about the data

Where the numbers come from.

Data sources

  • Google Analytics 4
    Sessions, channels, landing pages, ecommerce events for ankarsrum.com/us/
  • Google Search Console
    Organic search queries and ranking positions
  • Meta Ads
    Spend, reach, conversions for the Ankarsrum US account, via Windsor
  • WooCommerce
    Transaction-level data via GA4 ecommerce events

What to know about the numbers

  • The measurement period covers 15 May 2025 to 14 May 2026, one full season including holiday peaks.
  • Conversion values report subtotal excluding sales tax and shipping. Net, not gross.
  • GA4 uses last non-direct attribution. Meta and Google Ads use their own internal models, which produce different totals for the same purchases.
  • The US Meta account is billed centrally in SEK. The 34x ROAS figure refers to revenue attributed by Meta against spend, in the platform's own reporting.
